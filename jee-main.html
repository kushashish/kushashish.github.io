What’s new in JEE Main 2019?

  • Starting 2019, aspirants will be able to sit twice a year for JEE Main.
  • From 2019, JEE Main will be held in January and April.
  • Candidates will be eligible for taking examinations both times a year and their better score will be considered for admission.
  • The session will begin in August.
  • The exam will be conducted by the newly formed National Testing Agency (NTA).
  • From 2017, There shall be no weightage for the 12th class marks in calculating the ranks in the JEE Main.
  • For the candidates to qualify for admission in the IITs/NITs/IIITs and such other CFTIs whose admissions are based on the JEE (Advanced)/JEE (Main) ranks, they should have secured at least 75% marks in the 12th class examination, or be in the top 20 percentile in the 12th class examination conducted by the respective Boards. For SC/ST students the qualifying marks would be 65% in the 12th class examination.
  • Digital/scanned copy of the signature of Father/Mother/Guardian of the candidates has to be uploaded in the application form.
  • In the criteria set for the rank calculation, no weightage will be assigned to the marks obtained in the class 12th marks. This means the calculation of rank will be done purely on the basis of the marks scored by the candidates in JEE Main 2019.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Candidates who are born on or after October 1, 1994 are eligible to apply for JEE Main 2019 exam.
  • Candidates belonging to Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST) and Persons with Disabilities (PwD), must have been born on or after October 1, 1989 to be eligible for JEE Main. Candidates of these categories have been given a relaxation of 5 years.
  • Date of birth will be considered as mentioned in the Secondary Education Board/ University certificate.
  • It is mandatory for Candidates to have studied at least 5 subjects in 10+2.
  • Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics are compulsory subjects.
  • General category candidates require a minimum score of at least 75% or should be in the top 20 percentile in their respective 12th board or equivalent examination.
  • For SC or ST candidate, the minimum score is 65%.
  • Registered Aadhar Card Number is mandatory to apply for JEE Main exam.
  • Candidates can attempt JEE Main a maximum of 3 times.
  • Candidates can attempt JEE Advanced only for 2 consecutive years.

How to Apply?

  • Please read the procedure and information bulletin carefully before you start filling the Application Form.
  • Selection of date/slot of Computer Based Examination of Paper-1.
  • The mode of Application for JEE Main-2019 is ONLINE only. There is no OFFLINE mode of Application.
  • Please ensure your eligibility for the JEE Main exam as per the eligibility criteria laid down.
  • Each Candidate is allowed to submit only one Application Form. Multiple applications of a candidate are liable to be rejected.
  • Aadhar card is mandatory for application process.
  • The name of the candidate and his/her parents name in the application form must exactly be the same as registered in Class 12th/ equivalent qualifying Examination. No prefix/title such as Mr/Shri/Fr/Dr/Mrs/Smt/Col etc. should be used.
  • No options can be changed at a later stage after submission of an application. However, a chance may be given to the candidates to correct/modify some of the particular(s) of the application form online only, with valid reason(s), after one week of closing date.

Application Procedure

In order to appear in JEE (Main) and JEE (Advanced) 2018, the candidates are required to apply through online mode only as per the procedure described below:

Step 1: Applying Online and Submission of Application
  1. Supply all the required details of the Candidate while filling up the online form.
  2. On submission of details, an Acknowledgement Page with Application No. shall be generated.
  3. Candidates are required to take printout of Acknowledgement Page and keep it for future reference.
  4. The Acknowledgement Page is NOT required to be sent to JEE (Main) Secretariat.
  5. The application fee payment can be made by the following mode of Payments:
    • By credit/debit card/Paytm
    • By depositing fee in cash in the ICICI/Canara/Syndicate bank through e-challan generated during the online filling of application form.
Step 2: Uploading scanned images of Photograph and Signature
Size of the photograph must be greater than 4 KB and less than 40 KB.
Size of the signature must be greater than 1KB and less than 30 KB.
Note: The scanned images of photograph and signature should be in jpg/jpeg format only.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is new in JEE Main 2019? What is the syllabus, eligibility, language and other factors? Will they change?

The exam will be conducted twice a year i.e. in January and April and will be computer based. No, these parameters remain unchanged.

The candidates applied for both JEE Main paper 1 and Paper 2 but do not want to appear in Paper – 2 or Paper -1. Is this possible?

There is no compulsion for such candidates to appear in both papers, they can appear in the paper of their choice. But there is no refund of fee.

How many attempts can I give in JEE Main? Can I give it in 2020 also?

Candidates can attempt JEE Main a maximum of 3 times. Yes, you can give it in 2020 as well but the maximum allowed attempts are 3 only.

Is it mandatory to give the exam twice or can I give once? If I give the exam both times, which score will be considered?

No, this is the candidate's choice. Whether he/she wishes to give one attempt or two is entirely up to them. In case they have attempted both the exams, the better of the two scores will be used for the purpose of ranking in the merit list.

What is the difference between JEE Main Paper 1 and Paper 2? Is it mandatory to apply for both the papers?

JEE Main Paper 1 is for field B.Tech. and B.E. whereas Paper 2 is required for field B.Arch and B.Planning.
No, it is not mandatory. Depending on student’s choice they can choose papers or can give both.

In case photograph is not having name and date, would the form be rejected?

Name of the candidate and the date on which the photograph is taken is to be mentioned on the photograph, but in case uploaded photograph does not have name and date, the form will not be rejected and no correction is required in such a case and he/she will be issued admit card for appearing in JEE (Main) as usual.

I don’t have access to computer and no experience with online exams. How can I prepare for the exam?

Schools and colleges with internet and computer facilities in every region will be signed up for free practice sessions on every Saturday and Sunday. For JEE Main 2019 aspirants, this was started at the end of August.
